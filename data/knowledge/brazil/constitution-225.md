# Brazilian Constitution Art. 225 — Environmental Protection

**Source:** Constituição da República Federativa do Brasil, 1988  
**Chapter:** VI — Do Meio Ambiente (Title VIII, Chapter VI)  
**Relevance:** Constitutional foundation for all environmental regulation; drives EIA requirements for Caldeira.

---

## Core Provision (Caput)

> "Everyone has the right to an ecologically balanced environment, which is a public good for the people's use and essential for a healthy quality of life, imposing on the government and the community the duty to defend and preserve it for present and future generations."

This establishes the environment as a **diffuse right** — not owned by any individual, but protected for collective benefit.

---

## Key Paragraphs (§1)

### I — Preservation and restoration of ecological processes
- Government must preserve and restore essential ecological processes.
- Provides for ecological management of species and ecosystems.

### II — Genetic heritage
- Preservation of the diversity and integrity of Brazil's genetic heritage.

### III — Protected areas
- Government shall define territorial spaces requiring special protection in all federative units.
- Modification or suppression only by law; prohibits any use that compromises the attributes justifying protection.
- Relevant to Caldeira: APA (Área de Proteção Ambiental) surrounding the alkaline complex.

### IV — Environmental Impact Assessment
- **Mandatory EIA** for any installation of works or activities with potential to cause significant environmental degradation.
- The EIA must be publicized (RIMA — the public summary).
- This is the constitutional basis for CONAMA Resolution 01/1986 (EIA regulation) and DN COPAM 217/2017.

### V — Risk control
- Control of the production, commercialization, and use of techniques, methods, and substances that pose risks to life, quality of life, and the environment.

### VI — Environmental education
- Promotion of environmental education at all levels of instruction and public awareness of environmental preservation.

---

## Enforcement Provisions (§2–3)

- **§2:** Those who exploit mineral resources must **restore the degraded environment** per technical solution approved by the competent public authority.
  - Directly applicable to mining operations — mine closure and rehabilitation plans are constitutionally mandated.

- **§3:** Conduct and activities harmful to the environment subject **criminal, administrative, and civil sanctions**, with violators (individuals or legal entities) obligated to repair damage caused.
  - Strict/objective liability for environmental damage (no need to prove intent).

---

## Derived Principles

### Precautionary Principle
- Embedded in the constitutional duty to preserve for future generations.
- When scientific uncertainty exists about environmental harm, the burden falls on the proponent to demonstrate safety.
- Applied by MPF (Ministério Público Federal) in cumulative impact assessment requests for Caldeira region.

### Polluter Pays
- §2 and §3 together establish that the party causing degradation bears remediation costs.
- Informs CFEM royalty structure and mine closure bond requirements.

### Intergenerational Equity
- The phrase "present and future generations" codifies long-term stewardship obligations.

---

## Constitutional Hierarchy

Art. 225 sits above all ordinary legislation:
- CONAMA Resolutions 357/2005 and 430/2011 implement Art. 225's mandate
- DN COPAM 217/2017 implements the state-level licensing required by §1-IV
- Federal Law 6.938/1981 (National Environmental Policy) predates the 1988 Constitution but was received and strengthened by Art. 225
- ANM mining regulations must conform to Art. 225's environmental obligations
