# OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains

**Publisher:** Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development  
**Full title:** OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas (3rd Edition, 2016)  
**Supplement:** Supplement on Tin, Tantalum, Tungsten and Gold (3TG); increasingly applied to all critical minerals  
**Relevance:** Industry standard for mineral supply chain due diligence; referenced by EU Battery Regulation and U.S. SEC conflict minerals rules.

---

## The 5-Step Framework

### Step 1: Establish Strong Company Management Systems
- Adopt and communicate a supply chain policy consistent with Annex II (model policy)
- Structure internal management to support due diligence
- Maintain chain-of-custody or traceability system
- Strengthen engagement with suppliers — include DD expectations in contracts

### Step 2: Identify and Assess Risks in the Supply Chain
- Map the supply chain from mine to end-user
- Identify red flags (Annex II) at each point:
  - Armed group involvement
  - Forced labor, child labor
  - Human rights abuses
  - Bribery, money laundering
  - Tax evasion, non-payment of government royalties
- Assess identified risks against company policy and Annex II standards

### Step 3: Design and Implement a Strategy to Respond to Identified Risks
- Report risk assessment findings to senior management
- Devise risk mitigation plan:
  - **Measurable improvement** over a defined timeline
  - **Continued trade** with risk mitigation during improvement period
  - **Temporary suspension** while pursuing measurable improvement
  - **Disengagement** after failed mitigation or severe risk
- Implement the mitigation plan and monitor results

### Step 4: Carry Out Independent Third-Party Audit
- Independent audit of supply chain due diligence practices
- Audit scope includes smelters/refiners and upstream actors
- Audit standards: RMI (Responsible Minerals Initiative) RMAP, LBMA, or equivalent
- Audit reports shared with downstream buyers

### Step 5: Report on Supply Chain Due Diligence
- Public reporting on due diligence policies, identified risks, and steps taken
- Annual due diligence report published on company website
- Report shared with relevant authorities and industry initiatives

## Annex II — Model Supply Chain Policy (Key Provisions)

Commitments to neither tolerate nor profit from:
- Direct/indirect support to non-state armed groups
- Torture, forced labor, worst forms of child labor
- Human rights abuses
- Bribery, fraudulent misrepresentation of origin
- Non-payment of taxes, fees, and royalties to government

---

## Mineral-Specific Supplement

Originally focused on 3TG (tin, tantalum, tungsten, gold), now applied broadly:
- **EU Battery Regulation** (2023/1542) explicitly references OECD guidance for cobalt, lithium, nickel, natural graphite, and associated raw materials
- **Critical minerals industry** increasingly applies the framework to REE, lithium, manganese, and others
- **ISO 14001/45001** alignment: Environmental and OH&S management systems support Step 1 requirements
