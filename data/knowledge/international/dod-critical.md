# DoD Critical Minerals Procurement Requirements

**Authority:** U.S. Department of Defense; Defense Production Act (DPA) Title III  
**Key agencies:** Defense Logistics Agency (DLA), Defense Materials and Energy Agency (DMEA)  
**Scope:** Strategic stockpiling, domestic/allied sourcing mandates for critical minerals.  
**Relevance:** Caldeira's Brazil origin qualifies as allied-source REE for DoD procurement.

---

## Defense Production Act — Title III

### Purpose
Authorizes the President to ensure timely availability of critical materials for national defense through:
- **Purchase commitments** and **loan guarantees** to domestic/allied producers
- **Subsidies** for production capacity expansion
- **Stockpile** acquisition and management

### DPA Title III Critical Minerals Actions (Recent)
- **EO 13953 (2020):** Declared national emergency re: critical mineral supply chains
- **EO 14017 (2021):** 100-day supply chain review — identified REE as highest-risk category
- **CHIPS and Science Act (2022):** Expanded DPA authorities for semiconductor and critical mineral supply chains
- **DPA Title III awards (2022–2025):** Multiple awards for REE separation, magnet manufacturing, and battery material processing

## Allied-Source Requirements

### Berry Amendment & Specialty Metals Clause (10 U.S.C. §4862)
- Certain DoD procurements require materials from **U.S., qualifying countries, or allied nations**
- Qualifying Country List includes NATO allies, Australia, Japan, South Korea, and others
- Brazil is **not** on the Qualifying Country List for Berry Amendment purposes

### DFARS 225.7018 — Covered Materials
- Since 2021, DFARS restricts procurement of certain strategic materials (samarium-cobalt magnets, neodymium-iron-boron magnets, tungsten) to domestic or qualifying country sources
- **Waiver authority** exists for non-qualifying sources when domestic/QC supply is insufficient

### Allied-Source Framework (Emerging)
- **Minerals Security Partnership (MSP):** U.S.-led coalition (2022+) including Australia, Canada, EU, Japan, South Korea, UK, and others — Brazil invited as observer
- **IRA FTA-partner concept:** Brazil qualifies as critical mineral trade partner for §45X/§30D purposes under Treasury FTA-partner guidance
- **Bilateral MOU:** U.S.–Brazil Critical Minerals MOU signed 2023 — covers REE, lithium, nickel, graphite

## How Caldeira Qualifies

- **Brazil–U.S. Critical Minerals MOU:** Direct bilateral framework for REE trade
- **IRA FTA-partner status:** Brazil recognized for critical mineral sourcing under Treasury guidance
- **MSP alignment:** Brazil participates as observer in Minerals Security Partnership
- **Non-FEOC:** Caldeira's ownership structure has zero covered-nation involvement
- **NdPr supply gap:** Caldeira addresses the Tier 1 NdPr stockpile shortfall identified by DMEA
- **Separated oxide output:** Caldeira produces separated REE oxides (not concentrates), reducing DoD processing dependency on covered nations
